Los Angeles' best 8 drive guide to things to try in Los Angeles, California

Jill K. Robinson • 2 days ago

Cruising through the iconic palm-lined streets of Beverly Hills, Los Angeles.

Los Angeles is more than just a city; it's a collection of diverse experiences waiting to be discovered behind the wheel. From the sun-drenched coastlines to the historic mountain passes, the drive itself becomes part of the adventure. In this guide, we'll take you through the 8 best drives that showcase the true essence of Southern California.

1. Pacific Coast Highway (PCH)

There is no drive more iconic than the stretch of Route 1 that hugs the Santa Monica mountains. Starting from Santa Monica and heading north towards Malibu, you'll witness the raw beauty of the Pacific Ocean meeting the rugged cliffs. Stop by Neptune's Net for some fresh seafood and soak in the surfer vibes that define this coastline.

"The best way to see Los Angeles is through the windshield of a convertible at sunset." - Local Insight

2. Mulholland Drive

For those who love panoramic views of the city skyline and the San Fernando Valley, Mulholland Drive is the place to be. This winding road takes you through some of the most exclusive neighborhoods in the world, with numerous overlooks that are perfect for night-time photography.

Capturing the vibrant energy of a Los Angeles sunset drive.

3. Angeles Crest Highway

If you're looking to escape the hustle and bustle, head towards the San Gabriel Mountains. The Angeles Crest Highway offers a dramatic shift in scenery, with towering pines and snow-capped peaks in the winter. It's a favorite among local driving enthusiasts for its challenging curves and serene beauty.

The serene mountain views just a short drive from the heart of the city.

Why Driving Matters in LA

Unlike many other major cities, LA's layout encourages exploration. Every neighborhood has its own flavor, from the artistic enclave of Silver Lake to the glamorous streets of West Hollywood. Driving allows you to connect these dots at your own pace, uncovering hidden gems that tourists often miss.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the best time to avoid traffic?

Mid-morning (10 AM to 2 PM) and late evening (after 8 PM) are generally the best times to enjoy these drives without the heavy LA congestion.

Do I need a special car for these drives?

While a convertible makes the coastal drives more magical, any well-maintained vehicle will do. For the Angeles Crest Highway, ensure your brakes are in good condition.

Conclusion

Los Angeles is a city built for explorers. Whether you're chasing the sunset along the coast or finding peace in the mountains, these 8 drives offer a perspective of LA that is both intimate and grand. So grab your keys, put on your favorite playlist, and let the road lead the way.
